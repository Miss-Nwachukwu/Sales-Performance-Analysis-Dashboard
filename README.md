# 🧾 Sales Performance Dashboard (Tableau)

### 📊 Overview
Raw sales data rarely means the same thing to everyone who looks at it. A sales rep wants to know who's closing deals. A finance lead wants to know where the revenue is actually coming from. A marketing team wants to understand who the customer even is. And an executive just wants to know: *is the business getting better or worse, and what do we do about it?*

This project takes a single sales dataset and analyzes it through **four distinct stakeholder lenses**, each with its own questions and its own dashboard view:

- **📈 Sales Performance** — how are reps, regions, and channels actually performing against each other, and where are the gaps?
- **💰 Revenue Generation** — which products, categories, and pricing decisions are driving (or dragging) the bottom line?
- **🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Customer Behavior** — who is buying, how, and through which channels — and what does that imply about retention and targeting?
- **🧭 Executive / Business Performance** — pulling the above together into the handful of metrics and trends that matter for high-level decision-making and strategic action.

The goal wasn't just to build charts — it was to demonstrate that the *same* dataset can answer very different questions depending on who's asking, and that a good dashboard designer builds for the audience, not just the data.

**Dataset source:** Provided as part of my Aptech data analytics training, as a course-completion project to demonstrate applied understanding.

### 🧩 Dataset Highlights
The dataset includes:
`Product ID · Product Category · Customer Type · Payment Method · Region · Sales Rep · Sales Date · Sales Channel · Discount · Quantity Sold · Sales Amount · Unit Cost · Unit Price`

---

### 🎯 Objectives by Stakeholder

**Sales Performance**
- Track rep, region, and channel-level performance over time
- Surface top and underperforming reps and regions

**Revenue Generation**
- Identify which products/categories drive the most revenue and profit
- Measure how discounting affects sales volume vs. margin

**Customer Behavior**
- Break down customer types and preferred sales channels
- Spot patterns in how different segments buy

**Executive / Business Performance**
- Roll up sales, revenue, and customer signals into a single high-level view
- Flag trends and risks that warrant strategic attention

---

### 💡 Key Insights
- **Sales Performance:** top-performing regions and reps identified, alongside underperformers worth investigating
- **Revenue Generation:** discounting shows a measurable influence on sales volume, with certain products carrying disproportionately high margins
- **Customer Behavior:** channel and customer-type patterns reveal where the business is over- or under-indexed
- **Executive View:** sales trends over time reveal seasonality and growth patterns that matter more at the strategic level than the transactional one
